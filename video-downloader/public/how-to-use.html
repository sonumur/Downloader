How to Download Videos Online

The complete guide to saving videos from Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, Twitter/X, and more — in seconds, for free.

The Simple 3-Step Process

downloader.online works with a universal 3-step workflow for every platform. Once you understand these steps, you can download any supported video in under 30 seconds.

1

Find & Copy the Video Link

Open the app or website (Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, Twitter/X, etc.) and navigate to the specific video, Reel, or clip you wish to save. Look for the Share button — it typically looks like an arrow or paper plane icon. Tap it and choose Copy Link from the menu. This copies the video's unique URL to your clipboard.

2

Paste the Link Into downloader.online

Open your web browser and navigate to downloader.online. You will see a large input box at the center of the homepage. Tap and hold (mobile) or press Ctrl+V (Windows/Linux) or Cmd+V (Mac) to paste the link. Then click the blue Download button. Our servers will immediately begin analyzing the video to find the best available formats and qualities.

3

Choose Your Format & Save to Device

Within a few seconds, a results card will appear showing the video thumbnail and title. Use the format dropdown menu to select your preferred quality — options often include 1080p HD, 720p, 480p, and MP3 audio-only. Click the final Download button, and your file will be processed and saved directly to your device's default download folder — no watermarks, no sign-in required.

💡 Pro Tip: For the best video quality, always look for options labeled "HD", "1080p", or with an HD badge in the format dropdown. These are the highest resolution streams available from the source platform.

Available Quality Options Explained

When the video card appears after you paste a link, you'll see a dropdown menu with different format options. Here's what each option means:

MP4 Video Formats

1080p (Full HD): The best quality available for most videos. Recommended for saving to your phone or computer for later viewing. File sizes are typically between 10MB – 150MB depending on the video length.

720p (HD): A great balance between quality and file size. Half the storage footprint of 1080p while still looking sharp on most screens. Perfect for sharing on messaging apps or storing on older devices.

480p / 360p (Standard): Lower resolution options for when you need to save storage space or when your internet connection is slow. Still perfectly watchable on mobile phone screens.

MP3 Audio-Only Format

MP3 / Best Audio: Extracts only the audio track from the video. Great for saving music, podcasts, interviews, or any video where you only care about the sound. The audio is extracted in high quality (typically 128kbps – 320kbps).

📱 iPhone / iOS Users: If you are downloading on an iPhone, videos may open in the browser player first. Press and hold the video, then select "Save to Files" or "Save Video" to store it in your Photos or Files app.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

If you run into any problems, here are the most common issues and how to fix them:

❌ "Invalid or unsupported URL" error

This means the link you pasted is not recognized. Make sure you are copying the full link directly from the Share > Copy Link menu inside the app. Manually typed or shortened URLs may not work. Also double-check that the link starts with https://.

❌ "Unable to analyze this video" error

This usually means the video is set to Private or restricted. Our tool can only download public videos. If the account is private or the video has been taken down, it cannot be downloaded by any tool. Try checking the original post to confirm it's publicly visible.

❌ Video quality seems low

The quality options shown are all the formats the original platform makes available. If you only see 480p or 360p, it means the creator uploaded their video in that resolution. We always show every available quality — we never artificially downgrade the source content.

❌ Download button doesn't respond

Occasionally browser ad-blockers can interfere with our download process. Try temporarily disabling your ad-blocker and refreshing the page. Also make sure you have a stable internet connection, as the file processing happens on our remote servers.

❌ My downloaded file has no sound

Some high-resolution video streams (e.g., 4K or 1080p on YouTube-like platforms) are separated from the audio. When our system has access to FFmpeg merging, it automatically combines the best video + best audio into one file, labeled "HD". If a format says "(no sound)", choose a lower resolution option which is already pre-combined, or select the "Best Audio" MP3 option separately.
